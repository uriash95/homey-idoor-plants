Plants take up the water that they need from the soil through their roots. Carbon dioxide is a gas found in the air; plants can take in this gas through tiny holes in their leaves. Once they have water and carbon dioxide, they can use energy from sunlight to make their food. The leftovers from making the plant food is another gas called oxygen. This oxygen is released from the leaves into the air.

Take a look at the picture below to see this system in action:


This leftover oxygen is really important because humans and other animals need this oxygen to live which we breathe in from the air. When we breathe out, we release carbon dioxide into the air which is then used by the plants to make their food and the cycle starts all over again. This system is called the oxygen cycle.
